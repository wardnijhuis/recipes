Ingredients

  • 1 fairly thick slice crustless bread (white or brown)
  • 375ml milk
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 10ml butter
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp chutney
  • 1 tbsp smooth apricot jam
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tbsp brown vinegar
  • 1kg raw minced beef
  • 100g sultanas
  • 3 eggs
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt and turmeric to taste

Bobotie

Description

Bobotie is a South African dish consisting of spiced minced meat baked with an egg-based topping.

Steps

  1. Soak bread in milk. Heat oil and butter in large pan and fry onions and garlic.
  2. When onions are soft, add curry powder, salt, chutney, jam, Worcestershire sauce, turmeric, and vinegar. Mix well.
  3. Drain and mash bread, reserving milk. Add bread to pan along with mince and sultanas.
  4. Cook over low heat, stirring, and when meat loses its pinkness, remove from stove. Add 1 beaten egg, mix well, then spoon into a greased 28 x 16 cm baking dish and level the top.
  5. Beat remaining eggs with reserved milk (you should have 300ml, or a little more) and the salt and turmeric.
  6. Pour over meat mixture and add a few bay leaves on top. Stand the dish in a larger pan of water (important to prevent drying out) and bake, uncovered, at 180Â°C for 1 hour or until set.
  7. Serve with rice, coconut, chutney, almonds, and bananas.
